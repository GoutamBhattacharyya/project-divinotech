Enterprise Resource Planning

ERP is a modular software system designed to integrate and streamline the main functional areas of a company's business processes, into one unified system. It integrates management of core business processes such as Finance, HR, Distribution, Manufacturing, Service, Supply-Chain etc. ERP application also allows different departments to share information more easily with the rest of the company. It collects information about the activity of different divisions, making it available for the other departments where it can be used productively.

Team Collaboration

For an organization to run smoothly, the workforce needs to team up and work as a group. An effective management chain, right from recruitment-evaluation, work force management, to collaboration and knowledge based assistance for the employees, automates the business funnel and empowers the company to track and maintain the business processes.

Improved business processes

Relationship with an organization can be developed and maintained by providing good customer service. Formulating a means to serve better and provide good after sales service, is an ongoing process. Managing customer profiles, tracking orders, management of complaints, and feedback management are necessary features which should be present in the customer service of an ERP, to serve with ease.

Customer Service

Relationship with an organization can be developed and maintained by providing good customer service. Devising means to serve better and providing good after sales service is an ongoing process. Managing customer profiles, tracking orders, complaint management, and feedback management are the necessary features that should be present in the customer service of an ERP application, to serve with ease.

Deep Integration

In ERP, third-party integrations must be made to make the application work proficiently. These integrations can incorporate social media integrations and custom integrations. Deep integration prompts the analysis of various instances and makes the function of the application fluid and user-friendly.

Analysis

An ERP framework lets the organization examine each procedure that provides a bird’s-eye view, through real-time data analysis. By utilizing different analytical tools, the client can focus on business needs and advancement processes.

Data security

Data security consists of measures, which prevents unauthorized access to the system. It prevents accidental modification, destruction of data and from gaining administrative privileges. The major factor of data security is to block hackers from gaining access to databases and websites. It also safeguards data corruption. Every industry, big or small, considers data security to be an essential aspect of the business. The major ways of securing data are- - Data encryption - Data Masking - Data backup

Human Resource Management, Attendance and Leave Mechanism, Payroll Calculations and Distribution, Work Assessment, Rewards and Recognitions are the necessary tools of managing employees smoothly and getting the work done.

    Customized display of payroll, attendance, leaves, approvals, and appraisals for individuals is important for having transparency inside the system. Task management

The wireframe layout in the management system integrates the workflow by connecting different departments and allocating tasks accordingly. It helps to track employee performances and to make necessary changes from time to time, if required. Task scheduling and work group management become easier.

  • Work group managements

    It is essential to identify the skills, strengths and productivity of all the employees in a particular department. This is why an organization needs Workgroup Management System. Formation of teams considering their trait is the first step to achieve the best out of the available manpower. This also makes the work allocation easier.

  • Task scheduling

    An organization with various departments requires tasks to be precisely allocated, in order to move the wheel smoothly. Every individual has their own work limitations, and a good task management system allows the management to break down the requirements into small and executable tasks. It also gives them the provision of allocating those tasks to individuals, with the required skill set to execute them within the timeframe. Scheduling is one of the key features of the task management system. It creates transparency. A properly managed task management system acts as the central hub, having a continuous flow of tasks and also connects different departments and teams to collaborate and execute the daily tasks. Basic features of a task management system are

    • 1. Task / subtasks creations

    • 2. Scheduling

    • 3. Periodization

    • 4. Centrally controlled user permissions

    • 5. File attachment and notes

    • 6. Integration

    • 7. Reports

  • Time tracking

    Each task requires a specific amount of time, and every project must be completed by the due date. Time tracking enables individuals to verify the time taken to finish a task and amend it, if necessary. It helps to record the daily, weekly or monthly time logs of the employees, automatically in timesheets. It then sends the report for approval, generates invoices, and bills the client on time. It validates all work hours and makes sure that nothing goes overlooked. All of this, if done manually can put a lot of pressure on the company or an individual. Whereas a task management module, backed up with a robust time tracking mechanism, can provide all these solutions with a few clicks. It helps to

    • 1. Monitor ‘On time’ as well as ‘Overdue’ tasks

    • 2. Increases efficiency and productivity

    • 3. Helps in billing clients more accurately

    • 4. Helps to create a healthy competitive environment inside the system

    • 5. Analyze and calculate the efficiency of the team/individual

  • Alerts and reminders

    When an ERP application is incorporated with alerts and reminders, it notifies the user about an upcoming/overdue task or anything related to task scheduling and forwarding. This automatically notifies the user and other team members of changes in the working shift hours, request updates on specific rows, initiate a request for approval or sign-off on work items in the list. It also reminds the user when

    • 1. Key dates are approaching

    • 2. Today’s tasks

    • 3. Over due dates

A group-chat mechanism allows a group of users get associated with one another in real time. The members of the group can securely communicate with each other with ease. Streamlined operation

Enterprise Resource Planning (ERP) when properly executed can re-design the core business procedures, making them smoother and more streamlined. By initiating more proficient methods of activity to execute departmental operations, the organization can get a lift in profitability, quicker correspondence, and a decrease in the utilization of physical assets.

  • Lead management

    Leads are the building blocks of an organization, the right flow of which moves the wheel of other processes smoothly. Automated lead capturing, distribution to marketing channels, lead qualification, managing sales funnel, lead scoring, lead aging and lead lost are the features which are essential to run a successful Lead Management System. Managing lead is the first step to automate sales and marketing processes.

  • Procurement management

    In procurement, services are gained from an alternate association or firm. In ERP, Procurement Management System deals with managing suppliers, source planning, contract management, requisition, purchasing, payment etc. It likewise oversees supplier connections and manages transactions by recording payables to complete the source-to-settle cycle, by preparing supplier invoices.

  • Inventory

    Centralized storage management is essential for efficient stock operations to improve sales, productivity, on-time delivery, scheduling order frequency, planning, and forecasting with cost management. Basic features of an effective Inventory Management System are integration with procurement management, order scheduling, automated ordering, raw and finished products categorization, stock management and manual/barcode scanning/ radio frequency based stock entry. A stock management system gives the opportunity to manage waste and replacements, which cannot be overlooked by establishments dealing with physical stocks. Utilizing an inventory management system enables taking care of day-to-day undertakings. This results in more yields in less time and an increase in production.

    • Purchase to inventory

      Once the procurement/purchase gets integrated with the inventory management, it empowers the organization to keep up sufficient stock sizes, satisfy unique needs, purchase products at the best cost, and enable production forecast. This allows the organization to stock according to foresaw demand/risk, past performance, merchant or manufacturer incentives and more.

    • Replacement

      The replaced items can be restored with fresh/re-supplied stock. Once the replaced items are identified from the main stock, it becomes easier to understand the category to work with.

    • Finished product management

      From stock planning to stock package, distribution can be effectively managed with an integrated Finished Product Management module. Basic features of the Finished Product Management module are

      • 1. Production planning.
      • 2. Raw product consumption estimation.
      • 3. Actual consumption.
      • 4. End product inventory management.

  • Operations and logistics

    Once the lead and inventory are identified, those could be channelized through different processes of the establishment, like Follow Up, Documents Management, Approvals, Order Management, Logistics, and Sales & Invoicing. Logistics management enables opportunities like Order Management, Warehouse Management, Material Handling, Storage, Distribution, Grouping, Transportation, Fleet Management and Custom Duty Calculations.

  • Sales and invoices

    A Centralized Sales Management System makes sales more transparent and simple. With the availability of cost, quotations, discounts, and other data, the sales cycle becomes more productive and maximizes the achievements by reducing time and effort. Automated sales calculation makes the revenue/profit calculations flawless and provides fitting forecast. Printing invoices and the distribution of the same could be done whenever required, with the help of digitized invoicing mechanism.

  • Business Accounting

    Modules like creating ledger, journals and balance sheets have to be maintained simultaneously with other operations to reduce time and effort. An automated Business Accounting Mechanism reduces dependability on other resources by completing all the respective work. Such advanced solution can easily manage cash flow, budgeting, purchasing, bank transfers etc. The mechanism also provides the CSV file, in which GST is already calculated.

  • Waste Management

    If the complete ERP solution, which is based on product manufacturing/assembling and sales, is backed up with a Waste Management System, then waste categorization, stock entry, cost control, scheduling for reformation can be efficiently managed. A well established Waste Management Module can give valued input for the re-consideration of purchase decisions.

Order Fulfillment

Order fulfillment consists of all the processes which an organization undertakes, to fulfill the orders of a customer, while ensuring timely delivery. This process is directly linked with Customer Service, providing better customer satisfaction. The main functions of order planning are-Demand planning, Inventory management, Supply chain management, Logistics planning, Delivery management

  • Delivery config

    Delivery configuration lets the user set their delivery preferences like, time and place of delivery, payment mode etc. This lets the administrator set and deliver the request as scheduled.

  • Distribution channel

    The product and service distributions management module helps an organization analyze the movement of goods. It also helps to re-plan the supply. Once the distributions are cataloged in the system, the organization can channelize them in an efficient way for a cost-effective, time-saving and smooth distribution. Distribution channel helps distributors act as a value chain, through which products or services pass, until it reaches the consumer. Categorization of direct and indirect distribution channels, scheduling distribution of orders, and tracking them are the essential features of distribution channels.

  • Constraint management

    In the ERP framework, constraint management cautions the user about the consequences of future operations failure and alerts them to fix the issue beforehand. This prevents the factors which might cause loss to the business. Constraint management is also used by employers to ensure that employees are focused on their work process. There are two types of constraints

    • 1. External constraint

    • 2. Internal constraint

    External constraint is when the company’s production exceeds the market demand. In this case, the business must create demand for the specific product in the market. Internal constraint is when the production is less than the market demand.

  • Acknowledgement

    Acknowledgment management is a small but unique feature, which improves the relationship between the organization and end user. When the product/service is delivered, both the client and the administrator get an affirmation message. There are various types of acknowledgments like order confirmation, billing confirmation, shipment confirmation etc.

Return and Claims

The Return and Claims module manages everything, after the delivery is made to the client. This includes recording and reporting guarantee demands, receiving returned products, sending substitute merchandise, and favor or deny claims.

  • Claim and return form

    A Claim and Return form is an online form that must be filled out for every item that the client wishes to claim a refund or return. It serves as a record, which is further used for compensation and reconsideration. The mandatory fields in the form are

    • 1. Name of the customer

    • 2. Address of the customer

    • 3. Name of the product

    • 4. Product manufacturing date

    • 5. Product warranty period

    • 6. Reason for claim

  • Defect tracking

    The defects of the products are the updates given by the customer for refund or replacement. Defect tracking is the process of following the cataloged information about the defects of an item according to users, and making necessary amends.

  • Re-classification

    When a particular item is placed under a classification, and later its characterization is determined to be on some other level, re-classification occurs. Re-classification makes user search more precise.

  • Product renewal and discontinuation

    Product renewal depends exclusively on market request. When it is noticed that a particular product has picked up an unequivocal measure of achievement and the interest for that item has expanded, product renewal is arranged. When an item appears to be not fulfilling clients' needs, the production of that specific item is stopped. This leads to product discontinuation. Management of products through such categories makes the inventory identification precise and complete.

Custom Integration

Custom integration is the connection between the ERP system and third party, or other in-house applications, which are not already in the ERP’s directory. Custom integration gives the application a chance to enhance its capacities and capabilities. It makes the application easy to use and more responsive.

  • Applications and adaptors

    Of late, industries have evolved with unique and useful solutions. Establishments are depending on such solutions to perform specific tasks and achieving certain goals. A rigid and self-constraint ERP application does not make the whole process flexible. So, ERP applications need module based adaptors to integrate third-party systems in order to achieve a specific goal, to enhance the capacity of the process and to make it robust and versatile.

  • Enterprise service bus

    ESB is a set of standards and codes for coordinating various applications together over a bus-like framework. It incorporates diverse applications by putting a communication bus among them and enables communication between every application through the bus. This decouples frameworks from one another, enabling them to convey the information independently. On permission, an ERP can be easily integrated with the other required applications, to maintain the information flow and to centralize each and every process.

  • BI Integrations

    Business Intelligence leverages ERP solutions to transform data into actionable intelligence, which influences organizational strategic and tactical business decisions. Business Intelligence changes raw information into useful data, which helps the decision making process. It gives revealing information and also helps the user by letting them know what tools to be used for recognizing information groups, support for data mining systems, business execution management, and predictive analysis.

  • CRM integration

    The main purpose of a CRM application is to organize, track and manage the client’s entire information and work process. This helps in sales, marketing, and customer service. An ERP framework when incorporated with CRM, will give the organization better access to business data. It streamlines the business procedure as well. Above all, it gives the organization a 360 degree view of their client.
